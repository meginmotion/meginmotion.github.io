If you are going to worry about dying, there are many more probable ways to die than on a commercial jet.

Take a look at the chart below, which shows the chance of fatalities on a commercial flight compared to other causes of death in the United States. Notice that you are more likely to die from a bee sting than from a commercial flight. Whenever we fly, we have a one one-hundred-thousandth of one percent (.000014%) chance of dying!

Even if you were to get into a plane crash, surviving it may be the best thing that has ever happened to you. At the American Psychological Association’s annual convention in 1999, researchers from Virginia’s Old Dominion University presented some unique findings...     
Plane crash survivors were:

      
  • Healthier psychologically
    •   
  • Lived with substantially less anxiety
    •   
  • Less depressed
    •   
  • Suffered less from PTSD than travelers who had never been involved in an aviation accident
    •   

Here are few more tidbits about plane crashes that you might not know:

      
  • The first 90 seconds of a crash is known as “golden time”.
    •   
  • Planes routinely crash by running out of fuel
    •   
  • Survival rates improve when the pilot ditches
    •   
  • There is a 0.00001% chance your plane will crash (according to Professor Jeff Rosenthal) Not bad!
    •   

Next time you begin to focus on the possibility of something going wrong on a plane, think about the probability instead. Then you will have little to worry about.
